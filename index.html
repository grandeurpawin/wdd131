Official Flag of
Philippines

The flag of the Philippines consists of two horizontal bands of blue and red, with a white equilateral triangle at the hoist. Inside the triangle is a golden-yellow sun with eight primary rays, each representing a province. At each vertex of the triangle is a five-pointed golden-yellow star, symbolizing the three main geographical regions of the country: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. The blue color represents peace and justice, while the red symbolizes patriotism and valor. The white triangle stands for equality and fraternity among Filipinos.
